# Snapshot Stamp

The Snapshot Stamp verifies your participation in decentralized governance by confirming you have created a Snapshot proposal that received votes.

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Time** | ~2 minutes |
| **Validity** | 90 days |

## Requirements

- You must have **created a Snapshot proposal** on [snapshot.org](https://snapshot.org)
- The proposal must have **received at least one vote**

## Step-by-Step Verification

1. Navigate to the Passport App and connect the wallet you used to create Snapshot proposals.
2. Select the Snapshot Stamp.
3. The system will check your wallet for qualifying Snapshot proposals.
4. If a proposal is found that received votes, the stamp will be added to your Passport.

## Important Notes

- **Proposal creation required** — Simply voting on proposals does not qualify. You must have created a proposal.
- **Votes required** — The proposal must have received at least one vote to qualify.
- **90-day validity** — The stamp expires after 90 days and must be re-verified.

## Troubleshooting

- **Proposal not detected** — Ensure the connected wallet is the same one used to create the proposal on Snapshot.
- **No votes on proposal** — The proposal must have received at least one vote. A proposal with zero votes does not qualify.
- **Stamp expired** — Re-verify every 90 days to maintain an active stamp.
